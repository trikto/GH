# Greenhouse Landing Page Creation Guide

This document outlines the steps, structure, and design principles for building a beautiful and effective landing page for your greenhouse.

## 1. Goals & Target Audience
**Goals:**
* Attract professional retailers (like Cargills, Keells) wanting to showcase our products on their shelves.
* Attract local plant enthusiasts, gardeners, and landscaping professionals.
* Showcase your unique plant varieties, services, and greenhouse atmosphere.
* Drive foot traffic to your physical location or encourage online inquiries/orders.

**Target Audience:**
* Professional Retailers (e.g., Cargills, Keells)
* Hobbyist gardeners
* Landscapers
* People looking for indoor/outdoor plants
* Eco-conscious consumers

## 2. Tech Stack Recommendations
* **Core:** HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript
* **Framework (Optional but recommended):** React or Next.js for a more complex application, or simple HTML/JS with a bundler like Vite.
* **Styling:** Vanilla CSS or TailwindCSS. Vanilla CSS is great for custom, tailored aesthetics.

## 3. Recommended Structure (Sections)
A high-converting landing page flows logically. Here is the recommended structure:

### A. Navigation Bar (Header)
* Logo
* Links: Home, Our Plants, Services, About Us, Contact
* Call-to-Action (CTA) Button: "Contact Us" or "Shop Now"

### B. Hero Section
* **Visual:** High-quality, full-width image or subtle looping video of the inside of your lush greenhouse.
* **Headline:** Something inviting and fresh (e.g., "Fresh Leaves", "Organically Grown").
* **Sub-headline:** Briefly explain what you offer and where you are located (mention as upcountry hills).
* **Primary CTA:** "Explore Our Collection"

### C. Featured Plants / What We Offer
* A grid showcasing popular categories (Leafy Greens, Spinach, Cucumber, Gothukola, Lettuce).
* High-quality thumbnails for each category.

### D. About Us (The Greenhouse Story)
* A brief story about your passion for plants and horticulture.
* Emphasize local roots, sustainability practices.

### E. Testimonials / Social Proof
* 2-3 short, positive reviews from happy customers to build trust.

### F. Call-to-Action (Footer Preceder)
* Final CTA specific contact information.

## 4. Design Aesthetics
* **Color Palette:**
  * Primary: Earthy greens (Forest Green, Sage, Olive).
  * Secondary: Warm browns or terra-cottas.
  * Backgrounds: Off-whites or very light gray/cream to keep it looking fresh and natural.
* **Typography:** Modern, clean fonts. Consider sans-serif like `Inter` or `Roboto` for readability, paired with a classy serif for headings like `Playfair Display`.
* **Imagery:** This is the most crucial part. Use bright, well-lit, high-resolution photos of healthy plants. Avoid stock photos if possible; show *your* actual greenhouse.

## 5. Step-by-Step Implementation Guide
1. **Gather Assets:** Collect all high-res images, write your copy (text), and finalize your logo.
2. **Setup Project:** Initialize your project (e.g., `npm init vite@latest greenhouse-landing -- --template vanilla`).
3. **Build Core HTML:** Set up the semantic HTML structure (header, main, sections, footer).
4. **Apply Styling:** Implement your CSS design system. Focus on responsive design so it looks great on mobile phones.
5. **Add Interactivity:** Use simple JavaScript for things like a mobile hamburger menu, a simple image carousel, or smooth scrolling.
6. **Review & Polish:** Ensure all links work, check accessibility (alt tags on images), and optimize image sizes for fast loading.
